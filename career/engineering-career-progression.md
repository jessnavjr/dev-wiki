# Engineering Career Progression: Junior -> Engineer -> Senior -> Principal -> Architect

This guide outlines how progression typically works across software engineering levels, what day-to-day work feels like at each stage, and typical pay scales in Hawaii vs the broader U.S.

## How Progression Usually Happens

Career growth is less about title changes and more about increasing scope, complexity, and business impact.

1. **Junior Engineer -> Software Engineer**: You move from task execution to owning small features end-to-end.
2. **Software Engineer -> Senior Engineer**: You move from feature ownership to system-level ownership and mentorship.
3. **Senior Engineer -> Principal Engineer**: You move from team impact to multi-team technical leadership.
4. **Principal Engineer -> Architect**: You move from strategic technical direction to long-term platform and enterprise architecture.

## Compensation Notes (Read First)

- Ranges below are broad market estimates for **base salary** in **2026 USD**.
- Total compensation (bonus, stock, profit sharing) can be significantly higher, especially in larger U.S. companies.
- Hawaii pay is often lower than top mainland tech hubs, while cost of living can be high.
- Exact pay varies by industry, company size, remote policy, and specialization (AI/ML, security, platform, data, etc.).

## Level-by-Level Breakdown

### 1) Junior Software Engineer

**Typical experience:** 0-2 years

**What work is like:**
- Implementing scoped tasks with clear acceptance criteria.
- Pairing with more senior engineers and requesting frequent reviews.
- Learning codebase conventions, testing patterns, and deployment basics.
- Spending meaningful time debugging and learning tooling.

**Primary expectations:**
- Deliver reliable code for small tasks.
- Learn quickly and ask good questions.
- Improve code quality through tests and documentation.

**Pay scale (base salary):**
- **Hawaii:** $65,000-$105,000
- **U.S. (overall):** $75,000-$120,000

---

### 2) Software Engineer (Mid-Level)

**Typical experience:** 2-5 years

**What work is like:**
- Owning small-to-medium features from design through deployment.
- Translating product requirements into technical plans.
- Coordinating with PM, design, QA, and operations.
- Writing maintainable code and improving developer workflow.

**Primary expectations:**
- Ship with less supervision.
- Balance speed, reliability, and maintainability.
- Contribute to planning and estimation.

**Pay scale (base salary):**
- **Hawaii:** $90,000-$145,000
- **U.S. (overall):** $110,000-$170,000

---

### 3) Senior Software Engineer

**Typical experience:** 5-8+ years

**What work is like:**
- Leading architecture and implementation for major projects.
- Making design decisions around performance, reliability, and scalability.
- Mentoring engineers and raising team engineering standards.
- Handling high-ambiguity problems and production incidents.

**Primary expectations:**
- Own critical systems and outcomes.
- Influence technical direction for the team.
- Prevent classes of failures through better design and process.

**Pay scale (base salary):**
- **Hawaii:** $125,000-$200,000
- **U.S. (overall):** $150,000-$240,000

---

### 4) Principal Engineer

**Typical experience:** 8-12+ years

**What work is like:**
- Defining technical strategy across multiple teams.
- Driving platform consistency, architectural standards, and cross-team execution.
- Solving high-risk technical problems with long-term business implications.
- Coaching senior engineers and tech leads.

**Primary expectations:**
- Deliver multi-team impact.
- Align architecture with business strategy.
- Reduce organizational technical risk.

**Pay scale (base salary):**
- **Hawaii:** $160,000-$280,000
- **U.S. (overall):** $210,000-$350,000

---

### 5) Architect (Staff/Principal Architect, Enterprise Architect)

**Typical experience:** 12+ years

**What work is like:**
- Setting enterprise-level architecture direction and guardrails.
- Defining reference architectures, integration patterns, and modernization roadmaps.
- Balancing technical debt, security, compliance, and business priorities.
- Partnering closely with engineering leadership, product leadership, and sometimes executives.

**Primary expectations:**
- Create long-horizon architecture plans that teams can execute.
- Enable decision quality across the organization.
- Ensure systems evolve coherently across domains.

**Pay scale (base salary):**
- **Hawaii:** $150,000-$260,000
- **U.S. (overall):** $180,000-$320,000

## How to Move Up at Each Transition

### Junior -> Engineer
- Improve debugging speed and root cause analysis.
- Take ownership of one feature end-to-end.
- Write stronger tests and documentation without being asked.

### Engineer -> Senior
- Lead a project with cross-team dependencies.
- Make architecture tradeoffs explicit and measurable.
- Mentor juniors and improve team delivery quality.

### Senior -> Principal
- Influence roadmaps across teams, not just implementation details.
- Build reusable platform capabilities and standards.
- Demonstrate consistent business-impact outcomes.

### Principal -> Architect
- Formalize decision frameworks and reference architectures.
- Build executive-level communication around technical strategy.
- Drive multi-year modernization and risk reduction efforts.

## Practical Advice for Hawaii-Based Engineers

- Target remote-friendly mainland companies when appropriate.
- Benchmark compensation by role scope, not just title.
- Negotiate total package: base, bonus, equity, and growth path.
- Build visible impact artifacts: architecture docs, migration plans, incident retrospectives, and mentorship outcomes.

## Suggested Annual Career Check-In

Use these questions once per year:

1. Is my scope larger than my current title?
2. Can I point to business outcomes, not just completed tickets?
3. Have I multiplied other engineers' effectiveness?
4. Am I being compensated for actual impact and market value?
5. What is the single most important gap to close for the next level?
